What We Have Built

The MBA Series is a collection of 120 simulations (40 subjects, 3 use cases each) where students pick a senior executive role, analyse financial or strategic data, consult with AI boardroom agents, make a recommendation under time pressure, and defend it in an AI-powered cross-examination panel. Every simulation uses fictional Indian companies with realistic data, sealed role briefs with information asymmetry, and a 4-stage scoring rubric mapped to Bloom's Taxonomy.

Boardroom Mechanics

The boardroom is where the simulation comes alive. Three AI executives, each with a sealed brief containing data the student does not have, engage the student across 4 rounds. The intensity of this interaction is controlled by three independent levers that the student sets before entering.

Agent Behaviour
Guided: Agents volunteer information and help the student think through the problem.

Standard: Agents answer what is asked. Nothing more, nothing less. Push back when challenged.

Brutal: Agents have hidden agendas, withhold data, and will actively mislead if it serves their position.
Time Pressure
Relaxed: No timer. Think at your own pace.

Standard: 25 minutes. Enough if you stay focused.

Boardroom: 15 minutes. The board is waiting.

Crisis: 8 minutes. Breaking news. Every second counts.
Starting Information
Full Briefing: Complete background, all documents, full context.

Partial: Role brief only. Extract the rest from agent conversations.

Cold Start: One sentence. You figure out the rest.

Information asymmetry is central to the MBA design. Each agent holds data the student does not have. In Guided mode, agents offer it. In Standard mode, the student must ask the right questions. In Brutal mode, agents actively protect their information advantage and may mislead. This forces students to think critically about what they are being told and why.
