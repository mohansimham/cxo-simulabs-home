Before You Enter

Framework Primer

These are the strategic frameworks the boardroom will reference. You don't need to memorise them, but knowing them will sharpen your arguments.

Porter's Five Forces

Competitive Strategy
A framework for analysing the competitive intensity of an industry. The five forces are: threat of new entrants, threat of substitutes, bargaining power of buyers, bargaining power of suppliers, and rivalry among existing competitors.
Airbnb contextHomeAway was acquired by Expedia for $3.9B. Booking.com launched Homes. Wimdu is growing 25% QoQ. Tujia has 300K listings in China. The VP Growth will argue the competitive window is 18 months. Porter's forces explain why.

Two-Sided Platform Economics

Platform Strategy
Platforms create value by connecting two groups (hosts and guests). Network effects mean each new host makes the platform more valuable to guests, and vice versa. The key metric is liquidity, the probability that a listing gets booked.
Airbnb context1.5M listings across 190 countries, but 60% of revenue comes from just 20 cities. Host acquisition cost is $47 with $2,400 LTV. Guest acquisition cost is $31 with $680 LTV. Superhosts (1.2%) generate 22% of revenue.

Ansoff Matrix

Growth Strategy
Maps growth options along two axes: existing vs new markets, and existing vs new products. The four quadrants are market penetration (low risk), market development, product development, and diversification (highest risk).
Airbnb contextExpanding to 50 new cities = market development. Launching Experiences = diversification. The pilot showed 4.2x engagement and 20% gross margin vs 12% for accommodation, but diversification is the riskiest quadrant.

Marketplace Trust Architecture

Product & Operations
Marketplace trust is built in layers: identity verification, reputation systems, quality standards, financial protection, and dispute resolution. Each layer reduces friction but adds cost.
Airbnb contextOnly 31% of guests are ID-verified. Verified Photos covers 23% of listings but delivers 2.5x booking lift. Property damage claims are up 89% YoY. Host Guarantee paid $4.2M in Q3.

Technical Debt

Technology Strategy
Technical debt accumulates when short-term engineering shortcuts compound into long-term costs. Like financial debt, it charges "interest": slower development, more bugs, higher infrastructure costs.
Airbnb contextThe platform is a Ruby on Rails monolith from 2008. Response times degraded 40%. AWS spend is $18M/year growing 60% YoY. Mobile conversion is 2.1% vs 8.7% desktop.

Regulatory Strategy Framework

Public Policy
When facing regulatory threats, companies have four options: fight (legal challenge/lobbying), comply (adapt), partner (shape rules with regulators), or pivot (shift the business model).
Airbnb contextNYC's proposed bill could eliminate 30% of NYC listings, that's 12% of global revenue. SF registration compliance is at 20%. Barcelona is actively fining hosts. Paris is imposing 120-day caps.
How to use these in the boardroomThe AI agents will reference these frameworks implicitly. You'll score higher on Framework Application (30% of your grade) when you use them explicitly. Name them, apply them to the data, and show how they inform your strategic priority.
