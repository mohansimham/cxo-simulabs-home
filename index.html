The Experience

What a Session Feels Like

You've studied the frameworks. Now step into a real scenario from a real company and put them to work.

01

You're in the room

You've learned about product strategy in a course. Now you're a PM at Netflix. A CMO at Airbnb. A CFO facing a downturn. The clock starts. You apply what you know.

02

Others are watching

Peers make different calls. You see their reasoning in real time. Suddenly you realise your gut instinct was wrong — or right, and you didn't know why.

03

AI tells you the truth

When the round ends, AI breaks down your decisions. It connects your choices back to the frameworks you've studied — showing you where you applied them well and where to go deeper.

How It Works

Five Phases. One Decision.

Every simulation follows the same rigorous structure. No shortcuts. No hand-holding. Your decision is final.

Phase 1
BRIEF

Study the case. Run the numbers. Complete the calculation worksheet.

Phase 2
CONSULT

Question AI agents in the boardroom. Extract information. Read the room.

Phase 3
DECIDE

Make your call. Write your reasoning. Lock in your confidence level.

Phase 4
PANEL

Defend your decision under AI cross-examination. No safety net.

Phase 5
SCORED

AI rubric grading. Debrief report. Certificate and badge.

Adjustable Difficulty

Three Independent Intensity Levers

Start guided your first time, then crank it up. Each lever changes the experience independently.

Lever A — Agent Behaviour
Guided — Agents volunteer information and help you think
Standard — Agents answer what you ask, nothing more
Brutal — Agents mislead, withhold, and have hidden agendas
Lever B — Time Pressure
Relaxed — No clock. Think at your own pace.
Standard — 25 minutes. Enough if you stay focused.
Boardroom — 15 minutes. The board is waiting.
Crisis — 8 minutes. Every second counts.
Lever C — Starting Information
Full Briefing — Complete background, all documents
Partial — Role brief only, extract the rest from agents
Cold Start — One sentence. You figure out the rest.
